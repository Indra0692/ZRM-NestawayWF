Safety & Security Interactive Guide

Introduction

Overview

This team works for the safety of the tenants and the owners' properties. They handle monthly rent defaulters and resolve Tenant-Tenant, Owner-Tenant, and Society-Tenant issues.

Rent Defaulter Cases

Step 1

The Recovery team proactively follows up with rent defaulters. If a case is identified, contact them with tenant details to check the status.

Step 2

Additionally, directly contact the tenant to obtain a quick update on rent payment and inform the owner accordingly.

Tenant - Owner Conflict

Step 1

Ensure both sides are heard before taking any action against the tenant.

Step 2

Involve the ZM and Sales team to understand the tenant's perspective.

Step 3

Represent the owner while the S&S team represents the tenant to facilitate a fair compromise.

Step 4

If the tenant is at fault, convince the owner to cooperate and have the S&S team issue a warning to the tenant.

Step 5

If the issue recurs, the S&S team, along with the Sales team, should proceed with eviction procedures.

Opposite Gender Dispute

Step 1

Opposite gender visitors are allowed under specific conditions: appropriate visiting hours, no overnight stays, no disturbance to co-tenants or society, and visits limited to common areas.

Step 2

Violations are serious. A warning is issued for the first offense; repeated offenses lead to eviction.

Step 3

Accusations require proof. Request evidence such as CCTV footage or a written complaint from the society. If unavailable, inform the S&S team to conduct a surprise visit.

Dispute from Associations

Common Causes

  • Non-payment of maintenance or utility bills.
  • Disobeying society rules.
  • Opposite gender visitor issues.
  • Disturbance to neighbors.
  • Unclean property maintenance.
